About Me

Nice to Meet You

Hey! My name is Sandeep and I'm currently a student at UC Berkeley. I am currently studying Applied Mathematics with a concentration in Computer Science, and a potential double major in Data Science.

With my current majors, many options have indeed opened up in terms of what I want to do, and so taking the best of both worlds from my fields of study, math has made me a strong analytical thinker, while my technical skills have also been able to develop quite strongly. So naturally, I am inspired by the possibilities that the new technologies can bring, while also being wary of the capabilities of machines to learn. Being able to analyze data and train artificial "brains" to think the same way has become a great interest of mine of late.

This is why I am hoping to be able to explore and understand stuff like this, while also being able to pursue my other hobbies and interests, and live a life with no stone unturned :)

Scheme Interpreter

I developed an interpreter for a subset of the Scheme language. I also implemented some small programs in Scheme. The interpreter consisted of Read-Eval-Print-Loop components, which parsed user input, evaluated Scheme expressions, printed the string, and looped, respectively

Implementing a Database

I built a small version of a relational database management system (DBMS) in Java, as well as a Domain Specific Language (DSL) which a user can use to make database queries. The language that was implemented was similar to SQL.

Energy Price Prediction

In this project, attempted to build a dynamic time series model that predicts energy commodity price trends by evaluating risks in developing any form of energy infrastructure, emphasizing long-term accuracy and adaptability to economic shifts. Created a model enabled for long term price preiction, using ARIMA.

Inference and Capital Punishment

I investigated the relationship between capital punishment and murder in the United States. I used hypothesis testing and bootstrap sampling to check if the existence of capital punishment and murder rates were correlated. Used NumPy and Python

Fujitsu Software Engineering Internship

I interned at Fujitsu Network Communications in Summer '17. Following an iterative design process and agile SDLC, I created a UI that displayed network configuration data (NETCONF logs). It was made to facilitate efficiently debugging customers' networks. Created using Python parsing scripts, REST APIs and frontend UI to fetch network data

DecidingForce

Used a web app to process information in thousands of news articles describing interactions between police and protesters during the Occupy movement. Aimed to create an AI that can understand dynamics between cities and movements, and recommend policies more likely to result in preaceful expression. Worked with GoodlyLabs in BIDS
